A Sunday Drive Through Lena

a photography portfolio


A Sunday Drive Through Lena is connected to much of my writing through theme. I write a lot about the South and try to paint an image of the unique space through my poetry. I think any of the photos here could have unintentionally been a scene from a poem. Sometime in February, I was driving back to school after being home for the weekend and the particular route I took went through Lena, Mississippi. I’d driven through the town before and thought this is perfect for a photography shoot. My favorite of the collection is Strays Run The Town. If I had to connect it to a poem, it would be astigmatisms and other sight problems, and, while it is a sad poem, it is connected to this photo in a bright-red-string-board way. The photo was not intentional at all: I had been walking through random abandoned buildings and gawking at windows for nearly an hour when 2 dogs began running between me, the buildings and the road. In Strays Run The Town, the dog is running to my mom, who was trying to get him out of the shot. Another favorite is Thank you …. This was taken at a church I drive by incredibly often and I never noticed the sign on the fence lining the graveyard. It’s so ominous. Asking people not to throw flowers into a graveyard belonging to a church that hadn’t been in use in much time. The thank you—elipses really tops off this vibe. It adds an air of finality to something already long gone. Taking the whole portfolio into account, it is something I am happy with. It is my first photography portfolio but I feel as though it isn’t entirely new because of its connection to my writing. Everything I create or capture comes back to Mississippi and this project—taken on a drive through the South—portrays that.

Taken on Canon EOS 90D. Febuary 2025. Savannah Massey.
